Structure:

We welcome learners of all skill levels and programmatic backgrounds. It is recommended, if you do not have a foundation in R, to attend an "Introduction to R/RStudio" lesson (see calendar for details) to help you get started.

Our Fall 2018 calendar is structured in a scaffolded learning style, meaning, lessons build on each other. As a group we will be working through Hadley Wickham and Garret Grolemund's book "R For Data Science."

Like R langugage, the book is open source and can be accessed online for free. Please do not feel pressured to opt out of a lesson because you are unable to attend a workshop. We are a community of learners: Instructors and peers will be happy to help you get caught up to speed.

To help with sustainability, we ask for student volunteers to help co-teach lessons during our regular lessons. This is a great way to add teaching experience to your resume! Students can volunteer to help lead a class by contacting an instructor during class or by reaching out to commodorgroup@gmail.com.

"R For Data Science" uses sample datasets for exercises (e.g., iris and cars datasets). To have a more "authentic" experience, we seek to use topical datasets to learn with. If you are a faculty or student who knows of an interesting dataset our group can collectively "hack", please get in touch.

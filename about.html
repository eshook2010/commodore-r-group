Background:

Vanderbilt University's R Working Group is established through the univeristy's library system. In this group, we seek to supply learners with a foundational knowledge of the R programming language. Vanderbilt's R Working Group is created by Meredith Broadway, Data Analysis Librarian at Walker Management Library.

Structure:

We welcome learners of all skill levels and programmatic backgrounds. It is recommended, if you do not have a foundation in R, to attend an "Introduction to R/RStudio" lesson (see calendar for details) to help you get started.

Our Fall 2019 calendar is not a structured in a scaffolded learning style, meaning, lessons will not build on each other. You are not required to have attended a previous lessons.

Please do not feel pressured to opt out of a lesson because you are unable to attend a workshop. We are a community of learners: Instructors and peers will be happy to help you get caught up to speed.

To help with sustainability, we ask for student volunteers to help co-teach lessons during our regular lessons. This is a great way to add teaching experience to your resume! Students can volunteer to help lead a class by contacting an instructor during class or by reaching out to commodorgroup@gmail.com.

Community Code of Conduct:

See our Community Code of Conduct. If you do not agree with the code, this group is not a fit for you.
